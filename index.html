Paul Schmitt

My research interests span the areas of wireless network systems design, and network measurement and performance analysis. A common thread in my research is in-depth characterization of real-world connectivity in resource-limited environments, and the use of this analysis to subsequently inform system designs. Through comprehensive analysis across all tiers of real-world telecommunications infrastructure, my research produces solutions that solve fundamental connectivity problems, and in particular those that that manifest in challenging network environments.

I'm a postdoctoral research associate at the Center for Information Technology Policy (CITP) at Princeton University and I work with Prof. Nick Feamster. I previously completed my Ph.D. as a member of the MOMENT lab at UCSB under the supervision of Prof. Elizabeth Belding.
